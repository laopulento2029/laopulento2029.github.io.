Swiftieverse:
Awards

The Red Carpet !

Taylor Swift has amassed over 700 awards and more than 1,600 nominations, solidifying her status as one of the most decorated artists in music history. Her accolades span across various prestigious platforms, including the Grammy Awards, American Music Awards, Billboard Music Awards, MTV Video Music Awards, and iHeartRadio Music Awards. Notably, she holds the record for the most wins in several of these categories, underscoring her enduring influence and exceptional talent in the music industry.

Notable Awards

Grammy Award for Album of the Year

Grammy Award for Album of the Year

58th Grammys: 1989 – Made Taylor the 1st woman to win AOTY 2x for her solo recordings

Grammy Award for Album of the Year

63rd Grammys: Folklore – In 2021, her 3rd AOTY

Grammy Award for Album of the Year

66th Grammys: Midnights – In 2024, made Taylor the 1st artist to get 4 AOTYs

American Music Award for Artist of the Decade

In 2019, Taylor's decade-long impact on music was recognized.

Billboard Woman of the Decade Award

In 2019, Taylor's influence on the music industry over the decade was honored.

MTV Video Music Award for Video of the Year

2009: You Belong With Me – Marked her first VMA win

iHeartRadio Music Award for Tour of the Century

In March 2025, the success of her 'Eras Tour' was recognized. However, she did not attend.

CMA Entertainer of the Year

2009, first female artist to win CMA Entertainer of the Year twice.

Brits Award – Global Icon

In 2021, Taylor was the first woman to receive the Brit Global Icon award.


Taylor and Kanye Feud at the MTV VMAs 2009

At the 2009 MTV Video Music Awards, Taylor Swift’s big moment was famously interrupted when Kanye West stormed the stage during her acceptance speech for Best Female Video. The shocking act left Taylor speechless and became one of the most infamous pop culture clashes of the decade. In the moment, it seemed like Taylor had lost—her celebration was overshadowed, and many questioned whether she truly belonged on that stage.

But over time, the narrative shifted. The incident drew immense sympathy for Taylor, boosting her visibility and cementing her as an underdog the world rallied behind. While the feud resurfaced in later years, Taylor’s career only grew stronger, leading to record-breaking albums, sold-out stadium tours, and recognition as one of the most awarded artists in history. In the end, what once looked like a defeat became the spark that helped fuel her rise—Taylor may have lost the battle that night, but she undeniably won the war.
