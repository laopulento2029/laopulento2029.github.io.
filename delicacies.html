Pampanga Delicacies

Sisig

A Kapampangan specialty made of chopped grilled pork, chicken liver, onions, and chilies, served sizzling with a crispy savory texture.
Source: Wikipedia

Bringhe

A festive rice dish cooked with glutinous rice, chicken, vegetables, chorizo, and coconut milk, similar to Filipino-style paella enjoyed at celebrations.
Source: Cookmunity by Ajinomoto

Buro

A fermented rice dish mixed with fish or shrimp, offering a tangy flavor and commonly paired with grilled meats and vegetables.
Source: Wikipedia

Atchara

A sweet and tangy pickled green papaya side dish, often served with fried or grilled food to balance rich flavors.
Source: Cook With Sousou

Kamaru

A unique Kapampangan delicacy made from sautéed or fried field crickets, known for its savory crunch and distinct taste.
Source: Kusina Secrets

Tocino

A sweet and tender cured pork dish, loved nationwide, with Kapampangan versions famous for their balanced flavor and soft texture.
Source: Pampanga’s Best

Puto Maya

A sticky rice delicacy usually eaten with ripe mangoes and hot chocolate, enjoyed as a breakfast or merienda treat.
Source: Wikipedia

Tibok-Tibok

A creamy Kapampangan rice pudding made from carabao’s milk and topped with latik, known for its smooth and rich sweetness.
Source: Wikipedia

Aurora Delicacies

Baler Suman

A sticky rice delicacy cooked in coconut milk and wrapped in leaves, popularly showcased during Baler’s annual Suman Festival.
Source: Studocu

Baler Longganisa

Aurora’s well-loved sausage known for its sweet and slightly tangy flavor, often brought home as a popular pasalubong.
Source: Foxy Folksy

Yellowfin Tuna

Locally known as tambakol, this tuna is commonly grilled or served kinilaw-style, highlighting Aurora’s rich coastal seafood offerings.
Source: Yummy

Adobong Pusit

A savory squid dish simmered in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and onions, widely enjoyed in seaside homes and eateries.
Source: Yummy

Ginataang Sugpo

Fresh prawns cooked in coconut milk with chili and vegetables, showcasing Aurora’s abundance of seafood and coconuts.
Source: Pinoy Bites

Pako Salad

A refreshing salad made with pako ferns, tomatoes, and salted egg, commonly served in local restaurants for its fresh taste.
Source: Foxy Folksy

Pakumbo

A sweet candy made from grated coconut and sugar, known as a simple yet flavorful Aurora pasalubong similar to bukayo.
Source: YouTube

Buko Pie

A creamy young coconut pie with a flaky crust, freshly baked in Aurora and favored by visitors for its comforting sweetness.
Source: Amiable Foods
